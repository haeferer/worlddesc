# fraAngelicoCoronation

## Title

Coronation of the Virgin

## Artist

Fra Angelico

## Identification

- date: ca. 1425-1450
- medium: poplar panel
- dimensions: about 2.09 x 2.06 m
- Louvre location: Salle 708, Aile Denon, Niveau 1

## What Is Shown

- the Coronation of the Virgin in the main register
- the surviving principal register of an altarpiece
- a predella with scenes tied to Saint Dominic, including the dream of Innocent III, the dispute and miracle of the book, monks served by angels and the death of Saint Dominic

## Talking Points

- 15. Jahrhundert
- religioese Andacht und geordnete Feierlichkeit
- Licht und Farbe als geistige Atmosphaere
- himmlische Harmonie statt dramatischer Erdenschwere

## What To Notice

- the image feels ordered, but not cold; its calm is devotional
- brightness and symmetry work together to create spiritual elevation
- the work invites slow looking rather than dramatic shock
- the Dominic-related predella opens a second layer of religious meaning beneath the radiant main scene

## Why It Matters

- Fra Angelico is especially useful when discussing painting as an instrument of contemplation
- the picture shows how Renaissance order can remain gentle, prayerful and luminous
- it helps distinguish sacred drama from sacred clarity: the painting persuades through harmony, not turmoil

## Comparison Hooks

- compared with Cimabue, sanctity here feels less monumental and more inwardly radiant
- compared with Giotto, bodily presence gives way to a more elevated devotional atmosphere
- compared with Uccello, order here serves worship and contemplation rather than martial construction

## Provenance Notes

- possibly made for an altar in the rood screen of San Domenico in Fiesole
- in place there before 1435 according to convent chronicle evidence cited by the Louvre
- taken to the Louvre with its predella in 1812
