# cimabueMaesta

## Title

Virgin and Child Enthroned with Six Angels (Maesta)

## Artist

Cimabue

## Identification

- date: ca. 1275-1300
- medium: tempera and gold ground on poplar panel
- dimensions: about 4.27 x 2.80 m
- Louvre location: Salle 708, Aile Denon, Niveau 1

## What Is Shown

- the Virgin and Child enthroned
- six standing angels around the throne
- a great gabled panel designed for solemn frontal presence
- an original frame with many painted medallions, including prophets, apostles and other holy figures

## Talking Points

- spaetes 13. Jahrhundert
- monumentale thronende Madonna
- Goldgrund und feierliche Frontalitaet
- stark byzantinisch gepraegte Bildsprache
- guter Ausgangspunkt, um den Abstand zu Giottos neuer Raeumlichkeit zu erklaeren

## What To Notice

- the scale: this is not an intimate devotional image, but a public, commanding presence
- the throne still reads more as a sacred sign than as fully believable space
- the angels create order and hierarchy rather than casual movement
- gold is not only decoration here; it lifts the scene out of ordinary earthly time

## Why It Matters

- this work sits close to the threshold before later Italian painting becomes more bodily and spatial
- it helps explain what Giotto changes: not holiness itself, but the way holiness becomes visible in weight, gesture and place
- the painting is useful for talking about how medieval altarpieces organize reverence through symmetry, hierarchy and radiant surfaces

## Comparison Hooks

- compared with Giotto nearby, Cimabue feels more iconic and less situational
- compared with Fra Angelico, the sacred order here feels more monumental and less luminous in a Renaissance sense
- compared with Uccello, this painting is much less interested in constructed perspective than in sacred frontality

## Provenance Notes

- formerly in San Francesco in Pisa
- moved through Pisa before entering the Louvre in 1812
- now displayed in the Salon Carre

## Guide Notes

Ein Museumsfuehrer kann hier besonders gut ueber Uebergang sprechen:

- von ikonischer Strenge
- zu frueherer Beobachtung von Raum, Gewicht und Gegenwart

Gute Anschlussfragen:

- Was macht dieses Bild eher feierlich als lebendig?
- Wie erzeugt Gold hier Heiligkeit?
- Was veraendert sich, wenn wir danach zu Giotto gehen?
